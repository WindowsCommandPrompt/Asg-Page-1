What are 'Personal Achievements'?

Personal Achievements refers to any achievements that can be done individually or within a group. An achievements that was attained due to full/partial participation by myself, regardless of who initiated it, are considered to be my 'Personal Achievements'.


The developer of this website, by the name of Li Zhe Yun, would also like to express his words of gratitude to anyone who has made these achievements possible.

Now let's look at some of them, shall we?

We will first take a look at the achievements that I have attained while I was in Primary School, followed by the achievements that I have attained while attending Secondary School. Please stay tuned for my achievements obtained in tertiary education.

Quick sum up

Quick sum up of some of my major milestone in Primary School
  1. Grade 2 Violin Examination (merit)
  2. What were some of my thoughts and feelings after getting certificate? Well, if you were to ask me one day, I would say, hmm.. contented? Because this certificate is like a little milestone in my life. I still remember those days whereby I was constantly going off-beat and had to rely on my very own metronome to help prevent me from going off-beat. It was a real challenge back then! But I still managed to overcome any difficulties, and passed my Grade 2 Violin Examination with a merit.
  3. Grade 5 Violin Examination (merit)
  4. Grade 5 Violin Examination was the next music examination that I took after my Grade 2 Violin Examination in the year 2012. The song pieces that I had to play during my Examination was significantly harder as compared the exam pieces that I had played during my Grade 2 Violin Examination all the way back in the year 2012.
  5. Completed 400m swimming Examination
  6. I would say it was a rather interesting experience for me, though it was quite tiring having to spend long hours at the pool preparing for the upcoming swimming examination. But yeah, I still made it in the end. I have managed to pass a 200m swimming examination earlier on, but I am not going to put it up in the webpage this time round.
  7. Participated in Our People Our Music organised by Singapore Chinese Orchestra in 2014
  8. I still remembered that I was really struggling at playing the erhu,reading numbers on a score was rather a challenge for me, due to fact that I was lacking knowledge in terms of what do these numbers mean on the score, and what notes should I play on my instrument upon seeing that number. But, but, with time and effort, I managed to master a song required to be played at the 2014 Our People Our Music event, though I cannot remember what that song that we need to play was, but I was nominated by one of my teachers for being outstanding, mastering the song within the shortest time possible.
Fun Fact: I actually broke 2 Guiness World Records together with the other performers of having the largest drum assemble and largest number of Chinese Orchestra Performers. (If you look closely at the certificate)
